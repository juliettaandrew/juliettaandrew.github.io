Local activities

Here is a list of activities and links to help you plan what to do! This information was all provided by a friendly local who knows the area.
 

 

There is some great local theater. Lots of fun.
https://phoeniciaplayhouse.com

And our local library has many events.
http://phoenicialibrary.org

Hiking information
http://www.nynjtc.org
http://catskillguide.com/hiking.htm

Swimming and fishing and tubing
http://www.towntinker.com

Fishing permit info and issuing locations: 
http://www.dec.ny.gov/permits/6091.html
http://www.dec.ny.gov/permits/95698.html
http://www.nyc.gov/html/dep/html/recreation/fishing.shtml

The closest lake is about three miles west on Route 28. 
http://www.belleayre.com/summer/lake.htm

Swimming holes: 
http://www.watershedpost.com/2015/take-dip-where-swim-catskills
http://www.visitvortex.com/magazine/Swimming-Holes-In-The-Hudson-Valley
http://www.newyorkupstate.com/catskills/2015/04/best_swimming_holes_in_the_catskills.html

Also in Boiceville is Bread Alone, a renowned local bakery with a wide range of high-quality breads. 
http://www.breadalone.com/boiceville

There is a farm stand on the right side of 28, about two miles west of Boiceville, with really excellent local produce, sometimes including locally harvested wild mushrooms! Open from May to Novermber , 
http://migliorelli.com

A little further west, just before the junction of Route 28 and Route 212, is the newly opened Tremper Hill Farms. Nice selection of local and organic goods. 
https://www.facebook.com/Tremper-Hill-Farms-102570993425718/

Also, if you drive west on route 28, you'll pass Fleischmanns, which has two good Mexican restaurants, Arkville, which has a good weekend flea market, and the Margaretville, which has a full-sized supermarket (Freshtown) and nice stores and restaurants. 

Buying Local Food
http://www.watershedpost.com/2013/buying-local-food-users-guide

The 2015 paper and digital versions are available here!
http://www.watershedpost.com/2015/introducing-2015-catskills-food-guide
http://www.scribd.com/doc/255326215/2015-Catskills-Food-Guide

Farmers Market Database
http://www.buypurecatskills.com/farmersmarkets.html

The wonderful news in Phoenicia is Sweet Sue’s is open again! 
http://sweetsue1984.wixsite.com/sweetsuesrestaurant
https://www.facebook.com/pages/Sweet-Sues-Phoenicia-NY/105635942829235

In Phoenicia, Brio's for pizza, pasta, salad and other basics. Good, simple food and a nice family atmosphere.
http://brios.net/

The Phoenicia Diner is recently reopened under new management. Good, old-style diner food, but with good new touches and local flavors. 
http://www.phoeniciadiner.com/

The Peekamoose Restaurant is more upscale but the quality justifies the price. Child friendly. Reservations are probably a good idea.
http://www.peekamooserestaurant.com/

Pine Hill is a sweet little village about five miles west from the cabin. There is a new restaurant there that looks interesting.
http://www.zephyr-restaurant.com

In Woodstock, the Bear Cafe is an institution, where you may see famous people in a casual mode. 
http://www.bearcafe.com

Also in Woodstock, serving wonderful Middle Eastern and world food since 1972. 
http://joshuaswoodstock.com

Further afield is the Red Onion, which serves consistently good food.  
http://redonionrestaurant.com

The best sites for general news and activity listings in the area are: 
The Woodstock Times
http://www.woodstockx.com/

And the Watershed Post
http://www.watershedpost.com/

Firewood is sold in Phoenicia; please buy and burn locally to avoid transporting destructive insects.
http://www.phoeniciany.com
http://www.shandaken.us
